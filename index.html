Project Background

Why Lightweight AI-IDS?

Resource-constrained networks (edge/IoT) need threat detection that’s accurate and deployable. LAIDS explores architectures and compression techniques that keep performance high while shrinking memory and compute cost.

  • Motivation: real-time detection on edge-class hardware
  • Scope: supervised CNN/LSTM variants + unsupervised baselines
  • Constraints: memory, latency, energy
  • Evaluation: accuracy & zero-day generalisation at fixed FP budgets

Aims & goals

Overview

LAIDS investigates, develops, and evaluates lightweight AI-based Intrusion Detection Systems for resource-constrained networks, targeting strong detection and efficient deployment on edge-class hardware.

  • Benchmark CNN, CNN-LSTM/GRU, DBN, PCA-CNN, AE-MLP, and GAN variants
  • Evaluate quantisation (FP16, INT8 Dynamic, INT8 Full) and footprint
  • Assess zero-day generalisation at fixed false-positive budgets

Summary

Project Proposal

The proposal outlines objectives, candidate model families, compression/quantisation plan, datasets, and the evaluation approach for LAIDS.

  • Architectures: CNN, CNN-LSTM/GRU, DBN, PCA-CNN, AE-MLP, GAN variants
  • Compression: FP16, INT8 (dynamic & full calibration)
  • Metrics: per-class accuracy, ROC/PR, FP-budgeted generalisation
  • Deliverables: trained & compressed models + reproducible pipeline

General Model Pipeline

Preprocess Data

Clean, normalise, split; address class imbalance; extract salient flow features.

Train Model

Train CNN / CNN-LSTM/GRU and baselines with stratified folds and early stopping.

Test Model

Evaluate on held-out sets; track class-wise metrics and confusion trends.

Perform Quantisation

Apply FP16 / INT8 (dynamic & full); measure speed, memory, and accuracy deltas.

Test Generalisation

Zero-day style holdouts; compare at fixed false-positive budgets.
