Summary of Sian Caine's contribution to the LAIDS PROJECT

For my contribution to the LAIDS Project, I developed and evaluated two lightweight AI-based Intrusion Detection Systems optimized for resource-constrained networks:

  • A hybrid Convolutional Neural Network/Gated Recurrent Unit (CNN-GRU) model.
  • A hybrid GAN-MLP Generative Adversarial Network/Multilayer Perceptron model.

These models were benchmarked against a baseline 1D CNN-based IDS using the CICIDS 2017 dataset. To further investigate model efficiency, I also restructured the baseline 1D CNN into a 2D CNN to explore how introducing a second dimension would affect overall performance.

The results showed that both the 2D CNN and CNN-GRU models are viable for lightweight real-world deployment. The CNN-GRU achieved the best overall multi-class classification performance, while the 2D CNN excelled in detecting unseen attacks. However, the GAN-MLP model underperformed due to the limitations of its lightweight architecture.
